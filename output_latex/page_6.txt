\section{Theorem}
Assume \( \alpha \) increases monotonically and \( \alpha' \in \mathbb{R} \) on \([a,b]\). Let \( f \) be a bounded real function on \([a,b]\). Then \( f \in \mathcal{R}(\alpha) \) if and only if \( f' \in \mathbb{R} \). In that case,
\[
\int_a^b f \, dx = \int_a^b f'(x) \alpha'(x) \, dx.
\]

Proof: Let \( \epsilon > 0 \) be given and apply Theorem 6.6 to \( \alpha' \): There is a partition \( P = \{x_0, \ldots, x_k\} \) of \([a,b]\) such that
\[
U(P, \alpha') - L(P, \alpha') < \epsilon.
\]
The mean value theorem furnishes points \( t_i \in [x_{i-1}, x_i] \) such that \( \Delta \alpha_i = \alpha'(t_i) \Delta x_i \) for \( i = 1, \ldots, n \). If \( s_i \in [x_{i-1}, x_i] \), then
\[
\sum_{i=1}^n |\alpha(s_i) - \alpha'(t_i)| \Delta x_i < \epsilon,
\]
by (28) and Theorem 6.7(b). Put \( M = \sup |f(x)| \). Since
\[
\sum_{i=1}^n f(s_i) \Delta \alpha_i = \sum_{i=1}^n f(s_i) \alpha'(t_i) \Delta x_i,
\]
it follows from (29) that
\[
\left| \sum_{i=1}^n f(s_i) \Delta \alpha_i - \sum_{i=1}^n f(s_i) \alpha'(t_i) \Delta x_i \right| \leq M e.
\]
In particular,
\[
\sum_{i=1}^n f(s_i) \Delta \alpha_i \leq U(P, f') + M e,
\]
for all choices of \( s_i \in [x_{i-1}, x_i] \), so that
\[
U(P, f, \alpha) \leq U(P, f') + M e.
\]
The same argument leads from (30) to
\[
U(P, f, \alpha) \geq U(P, f') + M e.
\]
Thus
\[
|U(P, f, \alpha) - U(P, f')| \leq M e.
\]
